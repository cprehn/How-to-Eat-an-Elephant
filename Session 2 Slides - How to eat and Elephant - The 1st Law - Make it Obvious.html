Make it Obvious

You can't eat an elephant you can't see

  • We operate on autopilot 80% of the time
  • Awareness is the first step to change
  • Clear cues create consistent actions

Strategy 1: Habit Stacking

Link your new elephant bite to an existing habit

‚úÖ Formula: After [Current Habit], I will [New Habit]

  • After I pour my morning coffee, I will read for 2 minutes
  • After I sit down at my desk, I will write one sentence
  • After I put on my running shoes, I will text my accountability partner
  • After I close my laptop, I will put my phone in another room

Strategy 2: Environmental Design

Make the cues of good habits obvious and bad habits invisible

‚úÖ Good Habit Cues

  • Put books on your pillow
  • Lay out workout clothes
  • Put healthy snacks at eye level
  • Keep guitar in living room

‚ùå Hide Bad Habit Cues

  • Put junk food in pantry
  • Charge phone in another room
  • Uninstall social media apps
  • Remove TV from bedroom

Strategy 3: Implementation Intentions

Be specific about when and where you'll take your bite

‚ùå Vague Intention:

"I will exercise more this week"

‚úÖ Implementation Intention:

"I will exercise for 20 minutes by doing bodyweight exercises in my living room at 7am on Monday, Wednesday, and Friday"

"I will [BEHAVIOR] at [TIME] in [LOCATION]"
